Health Insurance Price Prediction Based on Demographic and Lifestyle Factors

Abstract:

Health insurance is a vital aspect of healthcare planning, offering financial protection against the cost of medical treatments and services. Predicting health insurance prices accurately helps individuals and families make informed decisions about their healthcare coverage. This study focuses on building a predictive model for health insurance prices using demographic and lifestyle factors such as age, sex, BMI, number of children, smoking status, and region.

Introduction:

Health insurance pricing is influenced by a variety of factors that contribute to an individual's health risk and potential healthcare expenses. Analyzing these factors can provide insights into how insurance premiums are determined and help individuals understand what drives the cost of coverage. This study aims to develop a predictive model that uses key demographic and lifestyle variables to estimate health insurance prices.

Data Collection:

The dataset used for this study contains anonymized information about individuals, including their age, sex, BMI, number of children, smoking status, and region. The data was collected from various sources, ensuring a diverse representation of the population.

Feature Explanation:

  1. Age: Age is a critical factor in health insurance pricing, as older individuals tend to have higher healthcare needs and expenses.
  2. Sex: Gender can influence health risks and healthcare utilization patterns, impacting insurance pricing.
  3. BMI (Body Mass Index): BMI is an indicator of body weight relative to height and is associated with various health conditions. Higher BMI values might lead to higher insurance costs due to increased health risks.
  4. Number of Children: The number of children can affect insurance pricing, as families with more children might have higher healthcare needs.
  5. Smoker Status: Smoking is a significant risk factor for various health issues, leading to higher healthcare costs for smokers.
  6. Region: The region in which an individual resides can influence healthcare costs due to variations in healthcare infrastructure, cost of living, and disease prevalence.
